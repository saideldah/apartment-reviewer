## ⚠️ What looks bad

### Main weaknesses

* ⚠️ **The yield is weak to average.** Gross yield is about **3.81% on asking price** and about **3.42% on estimated full acquisition cost**.
* ⚠️ **The price is not cheap.** At **€4,435/m²**, the listing sits about **5.1% above** the current ImmoScout snippet benchmark of **€4,220/m²** for Lichtenberg (Ortsteil). ([immobilienscout24.de][1])
* ⚠️ **It is already rented.** That limits flexibility from day one, especially for own use, aggressive repositioning, or strong rent upside. ([immobilienscout24.de][1])
* ⚠️ **No balcony and no fitted kitchen.** Those are not fatal flaws, but they do weaken future buyer and tenant appeal compared with stronger competing units. ([immobilienscout24.de][1])
* ⚠️ **Denkmalschutz is a real operational constraint.** Berlin’s official monument authority states that changes generally require a heritage permit, and that this can include windows, façade work, roofs, doors, and even some interior measures. ([Berlin.de][2])
* ⚠️ **The WEG risk is still a black box.** The listing gives you nothing useful on reserves, planned works, or potential Sonderumlagen. ([immobilienscout24.de][1])

### Why these matter

This is the classic problem with “nice-looking” Berlin investment flats: the public listing makes the unit look clean, but the real risk often sits in the **tenant contract, owners’ association finances, and legal restrictions**. When the yield is only average, those hidden risks matter more because you have less margin for error. ([Berlin.de][2])

* Payment history / arrears
* Whether the lease is indexed or stepped
* Any dispute, notice, or legal issue

**Why it matters:**
Because the flat is already rented, the real investment case depends heavily on what the tenant contract actually allows you to do. The listing alone is nowhere near enough. ([immobilienscout24.de][1])

---

### B. WEG and building checks

* Last 3 WEG meeting minutes
* Latest Wirtschaftsplan
* Latest Jahresabrechnung
* Current maintenance reserve
* Any discussion of Sonderumlage
* Planned work on roof, façade, windows, pipes, elevator, courtyard, heating distribution

**Why it matters:**
The attached energy certificate shows a **48-unit multifamily building** with a **2019 heat generator**, which is fine, but it tells you almost nothing about common-area capex risk. This is exactly where old-building deals can go wrong. 

---

### C. Legal checks

* Exact scope of Denkmalschutz protection
* Whether any Milieuschutz rules apply
* Land register issues
* Renovation restrictions
* Tenant-related legal limits
* Clarify the 1920 vs 1925 construction-year discrepancy

**Why it matters:**
Berlin’s heritage authority is explicit that you should **not start work without approval**, and that permit requirements can extend to major exterior works and also some interior changes. So this is not a property you should buy assuming maximum renovation freedom. ([Berlin.de][2])
